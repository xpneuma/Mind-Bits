Importance of Collective Intelligence (LLMs Ensemble) 

As a non-developer, AI has always been a reliable partner. I thought migrating the #OpenClaw channel from #Telegram to #Discord would be easy following the AI’s guidance. Instead, I spent days stuck in a loop because I trusted the AI too much.

Despite following the instructions from #Gemini (Pro and 2.5 Flash), errors kept recurring. Even after I provided the latest documentation, Gemini remained overconfident, claiming, "I've identified the issue; this will fix it." It didn't. This was a classic case of Self-correction Failure, where the model falls into a logical loop and repeatedly fails to fix its own mistakes. The real problem, however, was my own lack of doubt—I assumed Gemini would eventually solve it and followed its lead blindly.

Finally, I brought #Claude into the process. By cross-validating the steps between Gemini and Claude, the solution finally emerged.

This is not about which model is better; both #Gemini and #Claude are great. This struggle taught me why the industry is so focused on #Cross-model validation, #Ensemble of LLMs, and Multi-model #Orchestration. This #Heterogeneous Mix of models isn't just a technical trend; it’s a necessity.

The most important lesson here is that #Collective #Intelligence is just as vital for AI as it is for human decision-making. Reliability comes from a structure where different models check and balance each other, rather than the singular confidence of one.

For enterprises adopting AI and Agents today, asking "Which model has the best performance?" might be the wrong question.

The real questions are:
1) What is the actual #problem we are trying to solve?
2) What is the optimal #ensemble of models for that specific problem?
3) How do we #orchestrate that combination consistently and build a reliable #governance framework?
