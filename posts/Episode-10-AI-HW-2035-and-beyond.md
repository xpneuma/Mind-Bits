[ Mind Bits # 10 ] AI+HW 2035 and beyond

I recently read the vision paper "AI+HW 2035: Shaping the Next Decade (https://lnkd.in/gfH2sYmr)," and it provided me with deep insights and much to learn. The paper presents a compelling "Multi-layered Vision for AI+HW Co-Design and Co-Evolution" for the next ten years.

The 7 Pillars of the 2035 Roadmap:

(1) 1000x Efficiency: Shifting toward memory-immersed computation to maximize "intelligence per joule".

(2) Design Productivity: Using "AI-in-the-loop" workflows to bridge the gap between AI innovation and hardware design.

(3) Trustworthy AI: Building reliability through trade-offs among accuracy, robustness, and efficiency.

(4) Physical AI: Coupling data-driven learning with the laws of physics for real-time responsiveness in robotics.

(5) Unified Evolution: Focusing on compact, edge-ready models powered by memory-centric architectures.

(6) Global Impact: Coordinating efforts across the stack to manage the escalating energy demands of frontier models.

(7) Strategic Partnerships: Deep collaboration among academia, industry, and government to democratize compute access.

In the 2010s, software "ate the world," and in the 2020s, AI is consuming it at a lightning speed. Historically, AI faced several "winters" due to the computing limits of the time. Today, we have arrived at a similar junction where infrastructure, energy, and memory bandwidth have become our new physical constraints.

From "Wooden Barrel Theory" (Liebig’s Law of the Minimum), Hardware Technology is currently our shortest stave. No matter how high our algorithmic staves are, we cannot fill our "Intelligence Bucket" further without raising this hardware limitation.

While I don't believe AI itself will enter another winter, I do fear that companies or nations that fail to overcome this bottleneck may find their own competitiveness falling into a dark age.

What is more, Changing the Vessel Entirely What is truly fascinating—and what we must watch closely—is that we are not just looking at options to raise the shortest stave. We are witnessing a paradigm shift that could replace the entire barrel, for example:

‣ Quantum Leap: If quantum error correction is resolved, traditional physical limits of computation will lose their meaning.

‣ Space Infrastructure: Moving datacenters to orbit or the lunar surface could fundamentally solve energy and thermal dissipation issues.

‣ AI-Native Design: AI itself is beginning to design optimal hardware, creating a self-improving loop that accelerates problem-solving.

While the paper discusses a 10-year horizon, the current velocity of innovation makes this timeline shrink to 8 or even 5 years. Are you ready for the paradigm shift?
