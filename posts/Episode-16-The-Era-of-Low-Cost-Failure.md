The Era of Low-Cost Failure 🚀

I recently watched a career advice lecture by Professor Andrew Ng. (Video: https://lnkd.in/din5fCb7) Every word resonated deeply, but three messages in particular took me back to a passionate spring 10 years ago:
• "The people you surround yourself with are the strongest predictors of your success."
• "The cost of failure is much lower than ever before."
• "Work incredibly hard—lean in when you have the opportunity."

A decade ago, I was given a mission: "Predict business opportunities for 10 years into the future and create a technology roadmap." I had only 12 weeks.

Immediately, I focused on building the right team. I needed people who could predict the future, understand deep tech, and had a sharp sense of user experience. I formed a 'Dream Team' of diverse, proactive individuals across different ages, genders, and expertise. We worked relentlessly, often refusing to go home until we hit our daily targets. The sparks of collaboration and the sheer intensity of our brainstorming sessions were truly remarkable.

At that time, our team proposed 8 future scenarios and 11 new business models, including:
• ‘Human Galapagos Syndrome’: A society where people only interact with similar information and like-minded groups, leading to extreme isolation and a breakdown in communication without systemic help. (A strikingly accurate prediction of today's filter bubbles.)
• ‘Data Market’: A marketplace that mediates the buying and selling of personal data, recognizing its true value.

When we presented these to management, some called them "absurd," while others doubted if technology would ever catch up. Looking back now, the world we envisioned has either become a reality or is just around the corner.

While our team was deeply attached to these ideas, we couldn’t move them to PoC(Proof of Concept) stage. We had to settle for filing patents and each member returned to the original team.

But what if we were living in today’s ‘Agentic AI’ era back then? Instead of just submitting a proposal, we could have built an MVP (Minimum Viable Product) within days and pushed for a real-world launch.

This is why Professor Andrew Ng’s words hit so close to home. As he noted, the barrier to turning ideas into reality has never been lower. With AI as a powerful partner, the risk and cost of failure have plummeted. You might "waste a weekend," as he says, but you'll always learn something valuable.

If you have an idea, don't wait for permission. Execute it. Work with a relentless drive. If it succeeds, it’s a breakthrough; if not, that failure becomes the fuel for your next challenge.

To everyone navigating this era of rapid change—let’s keep pushing forward !
