The AI industry is moving at a breakneck pace this year—from the rise of OpenClaw to the recent announcements at NVIDIA GTC and Google’s TurboQuant this week. These breakthroughs are sending shockwaves through both the stock market and the tech sector.

As we enter the era of Agentic AI, the demand for computing and memory resources has exploded, leading to a massive supply-demand gap. In this context, the unveiling of Google’s TurboQuant at the upcoming ICLR 2026 has led some to believe that the red-hot growth in memory demand might finally cool down.

However, we must remember "Jevons Paradox":
"Technological progress that increases the efficiency with which a resource is used tends to increase (rather than decrease) the rate of consumption of that resource."

Think back to the evolution of audio and video codecs. In the early days, with limited network bandwidth, the goal was simply to shrink file sizes to meet basic consumer expectations. While this improved resource efficiency in the short term, it ultimately raised the bar for quality. As compression improved, consumers demanded higher-resolution audio and video, leading us to build even wider bandwidths and faster networks. Today, we routinely stream Lossless Media and 4K video.

TurboQuant, along with NVIDIA’s KVTC, will likely follow a similar trajectory. Instead of merely "saving" memory, these technologies will act as a catalyst for users to demand higher accuracy and higher-quality outputs through expanded Context Windows.

Rather than reducing the need for hardware, these efficiencies will drive us toward an era requiring even more computing power and memory to sustain massive, long-context intelligence. The current pause in demand is not a sign of contraction, but rather the precursor to an even greater expansion.

Ultimately, it all comes down to meeting consumer needs.
