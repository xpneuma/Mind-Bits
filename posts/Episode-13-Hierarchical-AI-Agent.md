In my last post, I shared how Context Drift—the tendency of AI to lose focus over long chats—can dull its edge. For an individual, we can mitigate this by simply "unplugging and restarting" or cross-checking with other AI. However, when we scale this to an enterprise environment, the stakes change dramatically, and the problems become more complicated.

We are moving from using AI as a tool to integrating it as a team player and strategic partner. In this AI era, issues like context drift don't just cause minor errors—they lead to Decision Contamination across the company, where flawed data results in flawed strategies. This is why #Governance and #Orchestration have become essential foundations of a company's Agentic AI infrastructure.

I believe the best way to use AI is to first look at how we solved problems before AI existed. Every company has its unique philosophy, vision, and problem-solving framework. We must "digitize" these human standards into our AI framework. If an AI understands your company’s core values and decision-making logic, it will become a real partner to make a value to the company.

A Hierarchical AI Agent framework is a great starting point to visualize this. Just like a human organization, it creates layers of accountability:

1) High-level Agents (The Strategists): They decompose complex goals into tasks and maintain the "Big Picture".
2) Mid-level Agents (The Managers): They coordinate between different teams and ensure alignment.
3) Low-level Agents (The Specialists): They execute specific skills, such as market research or financial coding.

A corporation is a massive, organic, and incredibly complex entity. Context Drift is just one of many issues we are facing as we integrate AI into the heart of our operations. The Hierarchical AI Agent Framework is also just one aspect of the resolution needed to solve these challenges.

Ultimately, the goal isn't just to adopt the latest technology, but to build a resilient intelligence ecosystem that reflects our organizational DNA. Navigating this complexity will require a holistic view of how humans and AI will work together safely and effectively.
