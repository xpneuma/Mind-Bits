It was an exhilarating journey yesterday as I dove deep into building my own "Mind Bits Model." Witnessing initial challenges transform into a symphony of technical triumph, where perseverance became the conductor, was truly rewarding. 🚀

As I watch people interact with OpenClaw, my mind is constantly racing with new ideas. I’ve been experimenting by creating sub-agents for different roles—developers, researchers, and project managers—to see how they collaborate.
We are still in the experimental phase, focusing more on problem-solving and defining the ideal multi-agent collaboration environment rather than delivering final project results. However, as I worked with these agents, one question kept bugging me:

"How can I ensure each agent consistently acts within its defined persona and reasoning style?"

To find an answer and test the limits of on-device AI, I decided to build a dedicated model for writing [Mind Bits] using #LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation) via #Ollama.

Due to the constraints of my #Chromebook (with only 4GB of available RAM), I had to set aside my desire for larger models and settled on #Qwen 2.5:1.5B. It was a minimalist yet strategic choice.

The process was a true "Ensemble" of efforts:
• Dataset Creation: I tasked OpenClaw with converting all 17 previous episodes into an Instruction/Input/Output structure, categorized by three traits: Philosophical Tone, Professional Insight, and Reasoning Style.
• Fine-Tuning: Using a script generated by Gemini, I ran the fine-tuning process on Google Colab with the OpenClaw-generated dataset.
• Deployment: I converted the final model to GGUF format and successfully loaded it onto my Chromebook.

I faced several bumps, such as JSONDecodeErrors and CUDA path issues—a true test of resolve. However, applying cross-validation between #Gemini and #Claude resolved them swiftly.

And here it is—today’s post was finalized with the help of my "Mind Bits Model"! It’s not perfect yet, but it’s a wonderful start to see what's possible.

I’m thrilled to wrap up the week with this new exploration. As more episodes stack up, the model will evolve through re-training. If this approach works, imagine building a "Dream Team" on-device, where every agent is fine-tuned for its specific role.
