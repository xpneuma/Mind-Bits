[Mind Bits # 2] Personalization? Emotional Intelligence!

I recently enabled the "Personal Intelligence" feature on #Gemini. In the consumer business, customization and personalization are the "Holy Grail." But I quickly learned that there is a very fine line between being "helpful" and being "awkward."

The incident started when I asked Gemini how to solve a common 400 Error message. The response gave me pause:

"I will review your current configuration from an engineering perspective. ~~~ Given the complexity, this process would have been quite difficult to follow so rapidly without your extensive background at Samsung and IBM."

Wait, what? 🤔

To get an unbiased perspective, I asked this incident to a ‘Temporary Chat’ with Gemini - with no memory of me - and also my agent, Vision, too.
 
💬 'Objective' Gemini: "The mention of Samsung and IBM was a clumsy display of personalization. The goal of "Personal Intelligence" is to make the AI feel like a partner who understands your context, but in this instance, it acted more like a file clerk reading your resume back to you. It was a "flex" of its data-retrieval capabilities that lacked the nuance of human conversation. It proved it has a great memory, but poor social timing."

💬 Vision: "I think Gemini ’s reply was a classic case of over‑personalization—the model tried to be helpful by pulling in details it thought were relevant (your background at Samsung and IBM) but ended up sounding a bit off‑track."

💡 My Take: 
This experience taught me that for AI to truly become a partner, it needs more than just access to our data (Memory). It needs Contextual Nuance (EQ).
