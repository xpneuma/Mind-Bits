 [ Mind Bits # 20 ] Benchmarking China’s AI Execution: A Wake-up Call

I recently read the Stanford HAI AI Index Report. While it was encouraging to see South Korea ranked 3rd in foundation model capabilities, the intense competition between the U.S. and China left me with a sense of urgency. Today, I want to share some thoughts on why we must stay alert and learn from China’s rapid execution.

A Scene from the Future: Shanghai’s Reality

My first trip to Shanghai was at the start of the mobile internet era. Driving from the airport, I saw futuristic skyscrapers mixed with traditional houses. It felt like walking into a scene from Blade Runner.
But what really surprised me was the people. Their openness to new things and passion for learning made it clear that they would become a major force in the global market.

The Power of Open Discussion

I have often asked people how China grew so fast. One reason that truly resonates with me is their communication style.
In Korea, our deep roots in tradition sometimes create a strict hierarchy in how we speak to others. This can occasionally make it hard to have free and comfortable debates, especially for my generation. In China, while they have their own social rules, their daily conversations are often more direct and horizontal. This environment allows people to argue and share creative ideas more freely, regardless of their position. I believe this "small but big" difference is a huge advantage for them in the AI era.

The Real "AI Factory" is Now Running

China has already integrated AI into everyday life—from mobile apps to e-commerce. Looking at cases like "The Crayfish Craze" (养龙虾) following the release of OpenClaw, it is clear that we have reached a point where we must benchmark China's speed.

The data shows why we should be concerned:
• Massive Token Use: ByteDance’s Doubao is now 3rd in the world for token use, right after OpenAI and Google.
• Heavy Users: While we are still exploring AI, many Chinese "heavy users" are already consuming 30M to 100M tokens every day.

China is already operating as a massive "AI Factory." To stay competitive, it is more important than ever to study their government policies, business strategies, and consumer trends.

[ Strategic Insight ]
"What we must learn from China is their ability for 'vertical integration.' They operate under a single national strategy that connects everything—from semiconductors and cloud infrastructure to final services. By 2026, the winner of AI supremacy will be decided not just by who has the best technology, but by who can integrate systems more efficiently into the real economy."
— Mustafa Suleyman
