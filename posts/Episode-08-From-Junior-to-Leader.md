Reflecting on my years as a junior engineer and later as a manager, I’ve realized that professional growth isn't just about years of experience—it’s about how quickly one adapts and delivers value. When I look at team members (and throughout my own career history), I see five distinct stages of growth:

(Stage 1) The Learning Phase: Requires multiple rounds of feedback to produce a basic result.
(Stage 2) The Reliable Phase: Delivers satisfactory results on the first try with clear instructions.
(Stage 3) The Proactive Phase: Not only delivers results but suggests constructive improvements.
(Stage 4) The Independent Phase: Navigates ambiguity to bring back high-quality solutions.
(Stage 5) The Leadership Phase: Takes initiative, proposes strategies, and executes independently.

This progression is essentially the journey from an entry-level contributor to a true leader.

Where does AI stand today? In this era of Agentic AI, it feels like AI is knocking on the door of the "Leadership Phase." However, in my view, current AI functions roughly at the level of a high-performing professional moving from Stage 2 to Stage 3, depending on the task—capable of incredible output but still requiring human "guardrails."

While AI is already replacing tasks in various sectors, the fear of total labor displacement remains a subject of intense debate. A recent insightful report from Citadel Securities offers a grounded perspective on this "intelligence crisis".

"Successive waves of technological change have not produced runaway exponential growth, nor have they rendered labor obsolete."

The report reminds us that technology adoption follows an S-curve, not a never-ending exponential explosion. Factors like the rising cost of compute and the complexity of human-to-human relationships act as natural boundaries to total automation.

We may move from "Inflated Expectations" to "Enlightenment" faster than ever before. AI is a powerful supply shock that expands our productivity frontier, rather than rendering us obsolete.

However, we must stay vigilant. Our goal should be to remain the "Ultimate Mentors and Leaders" to AI. By focusing on high-value, relational, and strategic roles, we can ensure that AI doesn't just find the most efficient path, but the one that creates the most value for humanity.
